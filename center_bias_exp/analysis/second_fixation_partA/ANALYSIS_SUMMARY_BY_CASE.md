**Key insight:**
- **Strong attractors (COM, CHC):** Fixations follow these centers even when displaced
- **Weak attractors (BBC, ICC):** When displaced, fixations ignore them and go to the remaining cluster

## 4. Context-Dependency Interpretation

The results demonstrate that **no single center universally dominates**:

1. **Overall:** COM appears to win (40.8%), but this is driven by specific contexts
2. **All-far cases:** Distributed bias - no clear winner
3. **Pair cases:** COM dominates in C1 and C2, but **ICC dominates in C3 (61.0%)**
4. **Isolated cases:** Strong hierarchy emerges:
   - COM and CHC are tracked when isolated
   - BBC and ICC are ignored when isolated

### Why ICC wins in Pair C3 but fails in iso_icc:

- **Pair C3:** ICC is isolated from CHC+BBC, but COM is nearby. ICC wins 61% because it has perceptual salience when separated from CHC/BBC.
- **iso_icc:** When ICC is displaced far from the other three (COM/BBC/CHC), fixations stay with the cluster (COM wins 53.6%), suggesting ICC lacks strong attractor properties on its own.

**Hypothesis:** ICC may serve as a "disambiguation center" when other centers form ambiguous pairs, but it does not independently attract fixations when isolated from the entire cluster.

### Main Findings:

1. **No single universal winner:** The "best" center depends on geometric context
