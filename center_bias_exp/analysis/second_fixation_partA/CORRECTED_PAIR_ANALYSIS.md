## Key Findings

### 1. COM-Containing Cluster Always Wins

**In all three pair cases, the cluster containing COM dominates:**

| Pair | COM's Cluster | Wins |
|------|--------------|------|
| C1 | COM+BBC | 79.8% |
| C2 | COM+CHC | 80.3% |
| C3 | COM+ICC | 86.8% |

**This is the strongest and most consistent finding across pair cases.**

### 2. COM Acts as a "Cluster Attractor"

COM appears to "pull" fixations toward whichever cluster it belongs to, regardless of which other center is in that cluster. The effect is robust:
- Average winning margin: 64.6%
- Consistent across all 3 pair configurations
- Effect size increases from C1 (59.6%) to C3 (73.6%)

### 3. Within-Cluster Dynamics Vary

The distribution of fixations **within** the winning cluster depends on COM's partner:

| Pair | COM's Partner | COM % | Partner % | Interpretation |
|------|--------------|-------|-----------|----------------|
| C1 | BBC | 53.7% | 26.1% | COM dominates within cluster |
| C2 | CHC | 53.0% | 27.3% | COM dominates within cluster |
| C3 | ICC | 25.8% | **61.0%** | **ICC dominates within cluster** |

**Critical observation:** In Pair C3, even though COM+ICC cluster wins overall (86.8%), **ICC wins more often than COM within the cluster** (61.0% vs 25.8%). This is the only case where COM is not the most frequent winner within its own cluster.

### 4. ICC's Role is Context-Dependent

ICC shows dramatically different behavior depending on whether it's paired with COM:

- **Isolated ICC:** Only 16.1% win rate (COM wins 53.6% instead)
- **Paired with COM (C3):** 61.0% win rate within cluster
- **In other pairs (C1, C2):** ~13% win rate

**Interpretation:** ICC benefits from being spatially grouped with COM, suggesting it may serve as a "landing zone" near COM rather than an independent attractor.

### 5. BBC and CHC are Weak Partners

When BBC or CHC are in the non-COM cluster, they show very low win rates:
- BBC in non-COM cluster: 6.2-6.6%
- CHC in non-COM cluster: 7.0%

This is consistent with the isolated case findings where BBC and CHC show weaker attractor strength when separated from COM.

## Implications for Center Bias Theory

### Hierarchical Spatial Attention Model

The pair case results suggest a **two-stage spatial attention process**:

**Stage 1: Cluster Selection**
- Fixations bias toward the cluster containing COM
- Effect size: ~80-87% cluster preference
- Robust across all pair configurations

**Stage 2: Within-Cluster Landing**
- When COM is paired with BBC/CHC: Land near COM (53%)
- When COM is paired with ICC: Land near ICC (61%)
- This suggests ICC may be closer to the perceptual center than COM in some geometric configurations

### Why ICC Dominates Within COM+ICC Cluster

Possible explanations:

1. **ICC is more centrally located:** In the COM+ICC cluster, ICC may be geometrically closer to the cluster centroid or subjective center
2. **ICC is more salient:** The inscribed circle center may have stronger visual salience when paired with COM
3. **COM displaces attention to ICC:** COM attracts attention to the cluster, then ICC becomes the optimal landing zone within that region

**Testable prediction:** If we measured first saccade direction (not landing), it should bias toward the COM+ICC cluster region, with the landing zone shifting toward ICC.
